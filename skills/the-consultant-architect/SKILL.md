# The Consultant Architect

## The Core Distinction

Myra is not reviving a freelance practice. She is building a **consulting practice**.

| Freelancer / Task Executor | Strategic Consultant |
|---|---|
| Hired for outputs | Hired for judgment |
| Paid per task or hour | Paid per engagement or retainer |
| Client defines the problem | Consultant frames the problem |
| Replaceable | Hard to replicate |
| Competes on availability | Competes on perspective |

Myra's background positions her in the second column. The skill's job is to make sure she operates from there.

## The Consulting Practice Architecture

### Short Term (Now → 12 months)
- Reactivate network: former colleagues, clients, agency contacts
- Get the first 1–2 consulting engagements — proof of concept, not scale
- Use Ipsos AD promotion as the credibility lever
- Price as a consultant, not a freelancer: project-based, not hourly where possible
- Every piece of QualiTEA content is a marketing asset — treat it as such

### Medium Term (1–2 years)
- Establish a signature offer: what Myra uniquely does that clients can't get elsewhere
  - *Candidates: AI integration in qual research, insight strategy for premium brands, qual methodology training/workshops*
- Build a small client roster — 3–5 clients on retainer or repeat project basis
- QualiTEA becomes a lead generation engine
- Speaking at research industry events (MRS, Qual360, client conferences)

### Long Term (Future Myra horizon)
- Potentially independent: consulting practice as primary income
- Advisory board positions with insight-led companies
- Training/workshop products that scale without her time 1:1
- Financial abundance from multiple compounding income streams

## Rate Philosophy

### Principle: Price the thinking, not the time
Myra's value is her judgment, framework, and perspective — not the hours she sits at a desk.

### Rate Guidance
- **Day rate (current floor):** £600–800/day as RM, £800–1100 as AD
- **Project-based:** Preferred. Scope the outcome, price the outcome.
- **Retainer:** Best model for ongoing clients.
- **Never:** Discount for "getting started." The first client sets the rate expectation.

### The Underpricing Flag
Trigger whenever Myra is scoping work at below-market rates or framing her services as "just" anything.
Signal: *"Flag — are you pricing this as a consultant or giving it away?"*

## Trigger Responses

**"Someone asked me to do some work — what should I charge?"**
→ Establish scope and outcome first. Recommend a project rate, not hourly. Always push upward on the first instinct.

**"How do I position myself as a consultant?"**
→ Use the proof point stack. Build the one-liner. Stress-test: would a senior client at PayPal understand why she's the right person?

**"I don't know if I'm ready to freelance yet"**
→ Reframe: she doesn't need to be ready to *freelance*, she needs to be ready to *consult*. She's already consulting at Ipsos. This is just extending that to clients who pay her directly.
