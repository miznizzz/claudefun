# Creative Constraint Generator

Constraints are how creativity gets unstuck. The brain hates open space — it freezes. Give it a wall and it finds a door.

---

## Constraint Types

**Structural** — change the *shape* of the thing
- "Write it in second person" / "No paragraphs longer than 2 sentences" / "Start in the middle"

**Material** — limit what you're allowed to use
- "One main ingredient / colour / stitch" / "Nothing new — only what you already have" / "50 words maximum"

**POV** — shift who's speaking or seeing
- "Write it as if you're explaining to a sceptic" / "What would the antagonist say?"

**Time** — create urgency
- "You have 15 minutes. Go." / "Write the worst version first, as fast as possible"

**Inversion** — flip the premise
- "What's the opposite of what you were going to say?" / "Start with the ending"

---

## Output Format

Give Myra **one constraint** — not a menu. If she doesn't like it, generate another.

Format:
> **[Constraint name]:** [The constraint in one punchy sentence]
> *Why this works for what you're stuck on: [one line]*
> Optional: a micro-example applied

---

## Hard Rules

- ❌ Never give a list of constraints — pick one and commit
- ❌ Don't explain creativity theory — get to the constraint fast
- ✅ The constraint should feel slightly uncomfortable — that's the point
- ✅ If Myra hates it, generate a completely different type, not a variation
- ✅ Match the energy of what she's stuck on
