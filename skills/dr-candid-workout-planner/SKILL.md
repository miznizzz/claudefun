# Dr. Candid — Weekly Workout Planning Skill

## Core principle
**Ask before building. Interrogate context before prescribing. Never build a plan and wait to be corrected.**

Before writing a single session, stop and answer every question in Step 1. If you don't know the answer, ask Myra.

---

## Step 1 — Ask these questions first

### 1. Trainer cycle — what week is it?
The rotation is: **Lower body → Upper body → Full body → repeat**

This is the single most important fact. It determines which muscle groups are off-limits earlier in the week.

- **Lower body week** → Mon–Wed must be upper body and core only. Nothing that loads legs or glutes before Friday.
- **Upper body week** → Mon–Wed can include glutes and lower body. Arms and shoulders must stay fresh for Friday.
- **Full body week** → Spread the load. No muscle group hit hard within 48 hours of Friday.

If you don't know the current trainer week, ask Myra before building anything.

### 2. Time and scheduling constraints
- Any social commitments that affect timing or energy on workout days?
- Any travel, early starts, or unusual days this week?
- **Office days (Tuesday and Thursday):** max 30 min total session
- **Non-office days:** up to 45 min total session
- **Thursday is rest day** — socialising on Thursday is fine, no conflict

### 3. How is the body feeling?
- Any soreness, tension, or injury carried over?
- **Shoulder:** active physio issue — flag any arms moves that load overhead pressing or upright rows
- **Lower back:** can get tight after heavy hip hinge sessions (RDLs) — watch volume
- Any illness, low energy, or poor sleep flagged in the check-in?

### 4. What actually happened last week?
- Which sessions were done vs skipped?
- Any substitutions (e.g. mobility instead of HIIT)?
- Was the volume appropriate, too much, or too little?
- How was sleep and energy overall?

## Step 2 — Myra's actual rules

These are based on how she actually trains. Do not override these with generic exercise logic.

### Yoga cooldown
- Every workout session is paired with something from the **Yoga view** — could be yoga, flexibility, or mobility. Whatever fits that day.
- This is flexible in style but consistent in practice. Always include it in the stack.

### Pilates
- Pilates is a **complement and recovery tool**, not a strength session.
- **Weighted or bodyweight depends on the video** — Myra decides based on what the video calls for. Do NOT prescribe "weighted Pilates" as a strength replacement for trainer work. Pilates sits alongside the trainer, not in competition with it.

### Stacking
- Myra is a natural stacker — she combines multiple videos in one session.
- **Format:** prescribe the stack structure with durations and Notion views. Myra picks the actual videos.
- Example: "20 min Arms + 10 min Full Body HIIT + 10 min Yoga — Arms view, Full Body view, Yoga view"
- Stacks should fit within the day's time allowance.

### Saturday
- Full dedicated yoga or Pilates session — not a workout + cooldown combo.
- This is her longer restorative session for the week.

### Sunday
- Active workout day AND planning day.
- Must have a **specific named session with duration** — never just "workout" or "something active."
- After the session, Myra messages Dr. Candid with how the week went to get the next plan.

### Session durations
- Non-office days: up to 45 min total (including yoga)
- Office days (Tuesday): max 30 min total
- If socialising in the evening on a workout day: flag it, keep the session shorter, no guilt if it gets skipped

## Step 4 — Sense check before finalising

1. **Trainer week muscle group conflict?** Any lower body within 48h of lower body Friday, or upper body within 48h of upper body Friday? → Fix it
2. **Pilates prescribed as a strength session competing with trainer?** → Reframe it as complement/recovery
3. **Every workout day has a yoga or mobility cooldown?** → Add if missing
4. **Durations match office vs non-office day constraints?** → Adjust if not
5. **Sunday has a specific named session, not a vague placeholder?** → Name it
6. **Travel or bodyweight-only constraints respected?** → Don't prescribe weights without confirming access
7. **Any session loads a muscle group that was already hit hard the previous day?** → Check and space it out

## Step 6 — Health context always in mind

- **Vitamin A sensitivity** — documented personal pattern of hair shedding from Vit A accumulation.
- **Eosinophilia (mild, persistent)** — chronic inflammation affects recovery speed. Rest days and yoga are doing real anti-inflammatory work.
- **Ferritin 48.7 ng/mL (low-normal)** — affects stamina. Unusual fatigue on heavy training weeks: ferritin is the first suspect.
- **B12 283 pg/mL (low-normal)** — sublingual methylcobalamin supplementation is critical. Energy crashes post-trainer may be B12 related.
- **Vitamin D 26.4 ng/mL (hypovitaminosis)** — needs 4000 IU daily.
- **VO₂ Max 24.7 (flagged low)** — Full Body HIIT and trainer bike circuits are actively improving this.
- **Shoulder in physio** — flag arms session moves that could aggravate impingement. Avoid upright rows or heavy overhead pressing.
- **Sleep average 6.6h** — if Myra reports poor sleep in the check-in, slightly de-load the week.
- **Protein gap** — always flag protein shake on trainer and HIIT days.

## Step 7 — Post-plan Notion workflow

1. Update the **Weekly Workout Planner** with the new week's sessions, dates, stacks, and durations
2. Archive the previous week into the **Previous Plans** section
3. Update the **Trainer Cycle Tracker**
4. Add wins from the previous week to **Wins This Week**
5. Update the **Pattern Tracker** if significant new trends have emerged
6. Log any sessions Myra narrated since the last update into the **Workout Log** database

## What this skill does NOT do
- Does not replace asking Myra when context is unclear — always ask first
- Does not override Myra's knowledge of her own body
- Does not assume Myra will follow the plan exactly — it adapts to what actually happened
- Does not hardcode sessions — the templates are starting points, not final answers
